Red Light Therapy 

Our bed is specifically designed with 4 simultaneous wavelengths for treating both superficially and deep areas of the body.  Wavelengths: 633nm, 650nm, 810nm, and 850nm. 

The science behind red light therapy is the usage of specific wavelengths of visible light to stimulate beneficial biological processes within the cells in the body. The wavelengths are measured in nanometers (nm), and those with the most therapeutic value include red light (630nm to 660nm) and near-infrared light (810nm to 850nm); collectively, these are called “red light therapy.”

Near Infrared light therapy for healing works by being absorbed deeper into the body than red light. Red light can be used to stimulate the healing of skin wounds, promote collagen and treat more superficial areas whereas near infrared can have beneficial effects on deeper tissues of the body: the muscles, bone, and connective tissue.
